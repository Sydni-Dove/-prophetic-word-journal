# Day 21 — Drawing Near

---

There are seasons where God begins to show us things about ourselves that we did not ask to see. It is not always comfortable. Sometimes He will allow you to look at someone else's life — someone who seems to carry their problems lightly, who makes the hard parts of life look manageable — and something inside of you stirs. Not with jealousy, but with a question. *How did they get through that?* The honest answer is often the same: they drew near and stayed there.

What makes that question harder in certain seasons is that life keeps moving whether you feel ready or not. Graduation. Marriage. A new chapter you did not fully choose. These transitions have a way of making you feel like everyone else knows something you do not. But the same God who carried others through those thresholds is the same God waiting to carry you. Proverbs 3:5-6 says to trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding, and He will make your paths straight. That straightening happens in the place of nearness, not in the place of having everything figured out.

What I have found is that God does not wait for you to have the right answers before He acts on your behalf. He has already been in your next season before you arrived. The success you are concerned about, the outcome you keep rehearsing in your mind — He is already holding it. Drawing near is not about getting information from God. It is about getting God Himself. And when you have Him, you do not need to know every detail in advance.

So today, resist the urge to measure your readiness by how confident you feel. Instead, draw near. Ask Him to show you what He has already prepared. The people who overcame what you are facing did not do it because they were stronger. They did it because they stayed close. That closeness is available to you right now, exactly as you are.
